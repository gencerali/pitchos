## Context

The site is an AI-assisted Beşiktaş news pipeline. Articles are

The concern: Google's review may flag the site as low-quality AI

This brief outlines what to audit, what to fix, and what to defer.
The goal: get to **85%+ approval probability** by completing the
P0 items below within one week, without disrupting the pipeline.

## Google's 2026 stance — what reviewers actually evaluate

Google AdSense policy (as of 2026) does not prohibit AI-generated
content. It prohibits **low-quality content regardless of how it
was produced**. Disclosure of AI use is not required but is
considered a positive E-E-A-T signal when framed correctly.

Reviewers evaluate (in roughly this order):

1. **Site-wide trust signals** — About, Contact, Privacy, Editorial
   Policy pages with substance
2. **Editorial polish** — does the site read like a real publication
   or like content factory output
3. **Author / editorial identity** — is there a real human behind
   this site, not anonymous AI output
4. **Per-page consistency** — is the worst 20% of indexed pages
   embarrassing or thin
5. **Originality and value** — does the site add insight beyond
   rehashing source articles
6. **Technical compliance** — ads.txt, mobile, page speed, no
   broken pages

Critically: **AdSense approves the whole site, not individual
articles**. Even great articles can be undermined by thin pages
(tag archives, short cards, social-quote articles) bulking up the
indexed count.

## Risk assessment — Kartalix specific

### P0 risks (fix this week)

#### P0.1 — Thin content indexed by Google
Per the PIPELINE doc:
- Articles scoring NVS 55-59 are saved as `rss_summary` mode (the
  source summary is shown essentially as-is, NOT placed in the
  live KV pool but saved to `content_items` DB)
- Twitter and Reddit source content can be very short (single
  sentences, scoreline tweets, "GOL 2-1!" updates)
- Template articles (T10 goal flash, T11 result flash) are
  intentionally short, time-sensitive cards

**The question**: are any of these publicly indexed at
`/haber/[slug]` URLs that Googlebot can crawl?

If yes → reviewers will sample them and see thin content. This is
the single biggest rejection risk.

**Action required:**
1. Run `site:kartalix.com` and `site:app.kartalix.com` on Google.
   Count indexed pages by type.
2. Audit `renderArticleHTML` and `serveArticlePage` — do they
   render `rss_summary`-mode articles? Twitter-source articles?
3. For any thin-content article type: either add `<meta
   name="robots" content="noindex,nofollow">` to its rendered HTML,
   or stop rendering it at a public URL entirely.
4. Confirm sitemap.xml excludes thin-content article types.
5. Audit T10/T11 templates — are they visually marked as
   live-event cards (badge, color, "Canlı" tag) so a reviewer
   understands them as time-sensitive cards, not as articles?

**Estimated effort:** 0.5-1 day to audit and add noindex headers.

#### P0.2 — About / Editorial Policy / Contact pages substance
Functions exist: `renderAboutPage`, `renderContactPage`,
`renderAttributionPage`, `renderPrivacyPage`. **Substance unknown
from code inspection.** A page that exists but is one sentence is
worse than no page in some reviewer interpretations.

**Action required:**
1. Open each of the four pages on the live site. Read as a Google
   reviewer would.
2. Rewrite About page (200-400 words minimum) with:
   - Real founder identity (Ali [full name or pseudonym], location)
   - Personal voice (Beşiktaş fan since X, started Kartalix
     because Y)
   - Mission statement (give fans news first, with context)
   - Explicit AI disclosure framed positively (see template
     below)
3. Rewrite Editorial Policy page covering:
   - How articles are sourced (RSS, social, API-Football, manual)
   - The role of AI in article production (drafting, scoring,
     rewriting)
   - The role of human review (editor checks before publishing,
     corrections welcomed)
   - Source attribution policy
   - Update / correction policy
   - Contact for editorial concerns
4. Contact page: real email address that's checked. Optional:
   physical location, social handles.
5. Privacy page: GDPR/KVKK basics + cookie/AdSense disclosure.
   Existing privacy page may already cover this — verify.

**AI disclosure template (suggested for About page):**

> "Kartalix is a Beşiktaş-focused news platform. Articles are
> researched and drafted with the help of AI tools, fact-checked
> against multiple Turkish football sources, and reviewed before
> publication. We attribute sources transparently and update
> articles as stories develop. Our goal is to give fans the news
> first, with the context to make sense of it."

**Why this matters:** "Disclosure of AI assistance combined with
clear human oversight" is a 2026 E-E-A-T positive. Reviewers are
trained to look for it. Hiding AI usage when output is obviously
AI-assisted is the worst option.

**Estimated effort:** 0.5 day if Ali writes the copy; 1 day if
including translation between Turkish/English and design polish.

#### P0.3 — Author byline / editorial identity on articles
Current state: unclear whether articles show a byline. If they
all show "Kartalix" or no author, the site reads as anonymous mass
content. This is a flagged pattern.

**Action required:**
1. Audit `renderArticleHTML` — what byline (if any) appears on
   articles?
2. Add a consistent byline: "Kartalix Editorial • Ali [Surname]"
   or similar.
3. Optional but stronger: link byline to an Author page
   (`/yazar/ali` or similar) with a real bio.
4. Add visible publication date and (where applicable) last-updated
   date on every article.

**Estimated effort:** 0.5 day.

### P1 risks (fix within 2 weeks, during review window)

#### P1.1 — Top articles audit
Reviewers sample, they don't read everything. The site's "average
quality from a 5-article sample" matters more than total volume.

**Action required:**
1. Identify the top 20 highest-engagement articles from the last
   30 days (use existing analytics or sort published articles by
   NVS desc).
2. Read each one critically as a Google reviewer would. Is it
   substantive? Does it add insight beyond what the source said?
3. For the bottom 5 of those 20 (the weakest of the "good"
   articles): improve them. Add analysis, context, depth.
4. The goal is that any random sample of 5 articles a reviewer
   clicks on includes at least 4 substantive ones.

**Estimated effort:** 1-2 days.

#### P1.2 — Article template visual distinction
Match-day cards, goal flashes, result flashes are legitimately
short — they're live cards, not analysis. But a reviewer skimming
URLs may not understand this without visual cues.

**Action required:**
1. Add a clearly visible content-type badge on each article:
   "Maç Önü", "Maç Günü", "Canlı", "Sonuç", "Analiz", "Transfer",
   "Haber"
2. Differentiate visual styling: live cards smaller, with different
   header color or icon
3. Long-form analysis should look distinctly like long-form (lead
   image, byline, structure, sections)

**Estimated effort:** 1 day.

#### P1.3 — Source attribution on each article
Reviewers check whether articles credit their sources. Failure to
attribute = "scraping" in reviewer terms.

**Action required:**
1. Audit `renderArticleHTML` — does every article show source
   attribution (e.g. "Source: Fanatik, A Spor" at bottom)?
2. For synthesis articles: list all contributing sources.
3. For single-source rewrites: clearly credit the original.
4. Where possible, link to the source URL.

**Estimated effort:** 0.5-1 day.

### P2 risks (fix when possible, not blocking)

#### P2.1 — Sitemap quality
Even with noindex on thin content, sitemap should only include
substantive articles. Sitemaps with thin content URLs signal
"please index this filler" to crawlers.

**Action required:**
- Audit `serveSitemap` — what's included? Should exclude:
  rss_summary articles, T10/T11 cards beyond 24h old, social-source
  articles, archived articles.

**Estimated effort:** 0.5 day.

#### P2.2 — ads.txt verification
AdSense requires `ads.txt` at `/ads.txt` with the right
publisher ID.

**Action required:**
- Verify `/ads.txt` exists and contains the Google AdSense entry
  with the correct publisher ID.
- Verify the file is served with `Content-Type: text/plain`.

**Estimated effort:** 15 minutes.

#### P2.3 — Page speed and Core Web Vitals
Slow sites get rejected. Worker-rendered pages are usually fast,
but verify.

**Action required:**
- Run Lighthouse / PageSpeed Insights on:
  - Home page
  - A long-form article
  - An admin page (less critical but shows polish)
- Target: LCP < 2.5s, CLS < 0.1, INP < 200ms on mobile.

**Estimated effort:** 0.5 day if fixes needed; 1 hour if just
running checks.

#### P2.4 — Mobile usability
AdSense reviewers test on mobile. Verify:
- All pages responsive
- No horizontal scrolling on mobile
- Tap targets large enough
- Text readable without zoom

**Estimated effort:** 0.5 day for fixes if needed.

### P3 risks (track, don't act unless rejection)

#### P3.1 — Backlinks / external trust signals
A site with zero inbound links from credible sources can feel
isolated to reviewers. Not blocking, but a signal.

**Action**: defer. If rejected, consider basic outreach to BJK
fan blogs / Turkish football communities.

#### P3.2 — Site age
9 May application means a known activation date. Site age isn't a
hard threshold but newer sites face stricter scrutiny.

**Action**: nothing actionable. Let time pass.

## What we are explicitly NOT doing

- **Don't make sweeping site changes during review.** Reviewers
  may re-crawl during the window. Changes that introduce new risks
  (new article types, new sources, new templates) could trigger
  rejection.
- **Don't apply to other ad networks in parallel** that place test
  ads. This can confuse the review.
- **Don't hide AI usage.** The 2026 stance favors disclosure with
  human-oversight framing.
- **Don't fake author identities.** Real founder name (or
  consistent pseudonym + clear About page) beats "John Smith"
  fake bylines every time.
- **Don't add a flood of generic "About" / "Privacy" boilerplate.**
  Reviewers spot template-only legal pages. Make them feel
  written by a real human.
- **Don't remove all short content.** Match-day cards have real
  fan value. The fix is visual distinction + noindex on the
  worst-quality, not deleting things readers care about.

## What happens after approval (or rejection)

**If approved:**
- Place ad units carefully — not in template cards or thin
  content, only on substantive articles
- Don't over-place. Aim for editorial-first, ads-second feel.
- Track per-page revenue to identify which content types monetize
  well

**If rejected:**
- Read the rejection email carefully — Google cites specific
  policy violations
- Focus fixes on the cited issue, not a generic overhaul
- Wait for recrawl (~2 weeks) before reappplying
- Reapplication after substantial improvement has good approval
  odds — first rejection is feedback, not permanent
